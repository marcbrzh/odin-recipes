Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a type of pasta, possibly one of the oldest types, made of very wide, flat sheets. Either term can also refer to an Italian dish made of stacked layers of lasagna alternating with fillings such as ragù, vegetables, cheeses, and seasonings and spices.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef
  • Onion, diced
  • Garlic, minced
  • Tomato sauce
  • Crushed tomatoes
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Egg
  • Olive oil
  • Italian seasoning
  • Salt
  • Black pepper

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions and drain.
  3. Sauté the onion and garlic in olive oil until softened.
  4. Add the ground beef and cook until browned.
  5. Stir in the tomato sauce, crushed tomatoes, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 15–20 minutes.
  6. In a bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, egg, and a portion of the Parmesan cheese.
  7. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce in the bottom of a baking dish.
  8. Layer noodles, ricotta mixture, meat sauce, and mozzarella cheese.
  9. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with sauce and mozzarella on top.
  10. Sprinkle the remaining Parmesan cheese over the top.
  11. Bake for 35–45 minutes, until bubbly and golden.
  12. Let the lasagna rest for 10–15 minutes before slicing and serving.
