Risotto

Description

Risotto is a traditional Italian rice dish known for its creamy texture and rich flavor. It is made by slowly cooking Arborio rice with warm broth, allowing the rice to release its starches and create a smooth, velvety consistency. Risotto can be enjoyed on its own or enhanced with vegetables, mushrooms, seafood, or other ingredients.

Ingredients

  • Arborio rice
  • Chicken or vegetable broth
  • Onion, finely diced
  • Garlic, minced
  • Olive oil
  • Butter
  • Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Dry white wine (optional)
  • Salt
  • Black pepper

Steps

  1. Warm the broth in a saucepan and keep it at a gentle simmer.
  2. Heat the olive oil and butter in a large skillet or saucepan over medium heat.
  3. Add the onion and cook until softened, then stir in the garlic.
  4. Add the Arborio rice and cook for 1–2 minutes, stirring to coat the grains.
  5. Pour in the white wine, if using, and cook until it has mostly evaporated.
  6. Add one ladle of warm broth to the rice and stir until absorbed.
  7. Continue adding broth one ladle at a time, stirring frequently and allowing each addition to be absorbed before adding more.
  8. Cook until the rice is tender but still slightly firm in the center, about 18–20 minutes.
  9. Remove from the heat and stir in the Parmesan cheese.
  10. Season with salt and black pepper to taste, then serve immediately.
